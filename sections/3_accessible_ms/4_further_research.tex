\section{Future Directions}
\label{accessible_ms:future}

The raison d'Ãªtre for even investigating Magidor forcing with collapses was to prove the following mutual stationarity result for $\aleph_{\omega_1}$:

\begin{conjecture}
  Supppose $V\models\mathrm{ZFC}+\mathrm{GCH}$, and let $\vec{U}$ be an $(\omega_1)$-Mitchell order increasing system of measures on $\kappa$, with $U_{\omega_1}$ Mitchell order above $\vec{U}$ giving rise to guiding generics for $\vec{U}$.
  
  Then in $V^{\mathbb{MC}\left(\vec{U}\right)}$, 
  \[
    MS(\seq{\aleph_{2n+3}\mid n<\omega} \frown \seq{\aleph_{\alpha+2n}\mid \alpha<\omega\text{ limit}, n<\omega}; \omega_1)
  \]
  holds.
\end{conjecture}

One possible idea would be to emulate the proof of $MS(\seq{\aleph_{2n+3}\mid n<\omega}; \omega_1)$ in \cite{koepke_ms_omega_1}.

Future work could include mutual stationarity principles of larger fixed cofinality or at every $\aleph_\alpha$ below $\aleph_{\omega_1}$, perhaps using supercompact versions of $\mathbb{MC}\left(\vec{U}\right)$.
